APPLIED STUDY OVERVIEW

The purpose of this document is to provide the student with an outline of what applied study of the trombone, euphonium, or tuba involves. The overview is broken into categories of ability in which a student will work through.  Each category contains goals to achieve and examples of repertoire and performance experiences that will help the student attain the set goals.  It is understood that each student at the University of North Carolina is an individual with different needs.  Therefore, it is expected that most students will work behind, ahead and between each category as they pursue applied study over four years.  This overview is a flexible document and can be molded to fit every student based upon their experience and ability at the time they begin their study.

 

 

Category I-Preliminary Work

 

Goals:

Development of Pedagogical Foundation

·        Listening!  Develop a sense of musical language-“song”

·        Develop sound concept and work to achieve on the instrument

·        Establish a daily routine of varying fundamental exercises

·        Develop relaxed approach to playing and performing

·        Constant focus on correct pitch and rhythm

·        Large/small ensemble

·        Lower level study of music theory

·        Lower level study of music history

 

Development of Practice Skills

·        Listening/Recording practice to develop critical ear

·        Schedule time for constructive practice

·        Prioritize goals in practice sessions

·        Consistency of correct approach

 

Gain Knowledge of Fundamental Weaknesses

·        Jury performance

·        Studio performances

·        Master classes

·        Recital

·        Regular recording of practice

 

Examples of Representative Repertoire

Scales: see scale syllabus

 

Etudes: Fink- Studies in Legato, Bordogni- Vocalises, Mueller-Technical Studies, Arban-Famous Method, Kopprasch-60 Studies, Tyrell- Progressive Studies, Blazhevich-Seventy Advanced Etudes, Clarke-Technical Studies, Snidero-Jazz Concepts, Coker-Patterns for Jazz, Aebersold-Playalongs

 

Solos: Guilment-Morceau Symphonic, Barat-Andante and Allegro, Rimsky-Korsakov-Concerto, St. Saens-Cavatine, Galliard-Sonatas, McKay-Sonata, Vaughn Williams- Six Studies of English Folk Songs, Mozart-Concerto for Bassoon,  Haddad-Suite for Tuba, Hartley-Suite for Unaccompanied Tuba, Bach-Air and Borree,  Pryor and Clark Solos

 

Options: Orchestral excerpts-“Basic 10”, aebersold playalongs, transcriptions of solo work by Charlie Parker, J.J. Johnson, Curtis Fuller, etc.

 

 

Category II-Intermediate

 

Goals:

Solidifying Fundamentals

·        Focused fundamentals routine

·        Regular recording of practice

·        Increased difficulty of repertoire

·        Critical and coherent evaluations of performance

·        Listening/diagnosing/finding solutions

 

Development of Musical Voice

·        Listening to great performances, live or recorded

·        Separation of fundamental practice and musical performance

·        Sing!

·        Advanced music history and theory

·        Collaboration with other musicians (i.e. solo w/piano, quintet, etc)

 

Performance Experience

·        Recitals

·        Large ensembles

·        Chamber ensembles

·        Seek out performance opportunities-paid or unpaid

·        Summer music festival or performance employment

 

Examples of Representative Repertoire

Scales: See Syllabus

 

Etudes:  Rochut-Vol I and II, Kopprasch-Vol II, Famous Method by Arban, Masson-12 Various Etudes, Blazhevich-Advanced Etudes, Bitsch-Rhythmic Studies, Bach-Cello Suites, Fritze- Characteristic Etudes, Aebersold-Playalongs, Coker-Patterns for Jazz

 

Solos:  Serocki-Sonata, Salzedo-Sonata, Concerto by Handel, Vivaldi, Jacob, Bozza-Ballade, Sulek-Sonata, Hindemith-Sonata, Horowitz-Concerto, Gillingham-Blue Lake Fantasies, and David-Concertino, White-Lyric Suite, Gregson-Concerto, Ewazen-Sonata, Arnold-Fantasy

 

Excerpts-“Basic 20”

 

 

Optional Pursuits

1.      Summer performance job (Disney, Busch Gardens, etc)

2.      TUBA and ITA competitions

3.      Music festival (Aspen, Mendez, Eastern etc)

 

 

 

Category III-Advanced

 

Goals:

Further Independent Work

·        Continued focus on listening and fundamentals

·        Full recital

·        Lecture recital

·        Advanced theory and history

·        Research

·        Chamber ensemble mandatory

 

Graduate and Professional Preparation

·        Recitals

·        Auditions

·        Solo competitions

·        International workshops

·        Freelance work

·        Teaching: privately or student teaching program

·        Instruction with other professionals

 

Examples of Representative Repertoire

 

Scales:  see syllabus

 

Etudes:  Bordogni, Arban, Bozza, Bitsch, Charlier and Maxted.  Solo work of Charlie Parker. Advanced Aebersold as well as Listening and Transcribing.  Memorization of all Important Excerpts, Memorize Standards

 

Solos:   All Major Works for the Trombone, Euphonium and Tuba.  Examples: Concertos of Tomasi and Rouse– Concerto of J. Bach, Rhapsody by Curnow – Concertos of V. Williams and Broughton
